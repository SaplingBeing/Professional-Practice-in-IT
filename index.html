What is it?

It was made to support whānau navigate the journey of mate wareware with confidence and aroha, destigmatising the condition.

Protection

Tiaki

Protecting Māori Mātauranga

The Tohunga Suppression Act 1907 criminalized traditional healing; this app counters that legacy by reclaiming and centering Māori understandings of wellbeing and mate wareware (New Zealand Legislation, 2026).

Safeguards whānau wellbeing by providing health information that is culturally appropriate, ensuring Māori are not forced to navigate systems that ignore their reo or tikanga.

Cultural & Health Protection

Digital & Health Equities

Historical policies have consequently created long-term disparities in health and resource access. By providing a culturally grounded digital tool, matewareware.nz actively works to close those gaps and provide the protection of wellbeing guaranteed under Te Tiriti.

Articles

Article 2 - Absolute Sovereignty
TINO RANGATIRATANGA

Māori having authority, autonomy, & stewardship over their own knowledge & resources (New Zealand Legislation, 2026).

Māori Perspective T he app is built from a Māori worldview, ensuring that mātauranga regarding mate wareware remains under the kaitiakitanga and authority of whānau. The Māori influence can be instantly seen upon intially loading the website since the user is greeted with a Karakia (Mate wareware, 2021).
Crown Perspective T he crown's duty is to protect Māori interests and support self-determination. By acknowledging and commending a Māori-led digital platform, the Crown moves away from a "one-size-fits-all" system and fulfills its obligation to safeguard indigenous knowledge, including autonomy in health.

Article 2 - Relation to the IT Industry

Article 2's messaging is prominent in how matewareware.nz is presented from a Māori worldview, as a dedicated web application was developed to safegaurd Māori authority, autonomy, & stewardship over the portrayal of mate wareware. The web application reflects the Māori understanding of health, ensuring Māori perspectives are embedded in how the information is conveyed.

Article 3 - Equity
Ōritetanga

Bridging disparities by transforming systems to achieve fairness in access & outcomes - ensuring equal rights & duties for Māori & non-Māori (New Zealand Legislation, 2026).

Māori Perspective T he application helps ammend low health literacy gap, educating the people how to detect early symptoms of mate wareware and how to address it.

Furthermore, the application ensures information regarding mate wareware is readily accessible (free) and considerate (due to incorporating Māori ideology), allowing whānau to care for their loved ones with ample support.
Crown Perspective T he crown promotes equality and fairness by supporting the development of matewareware.nz, as government endorsed partners like MedTechCoRE is cited within the footer.

Although the connection is somewhat indirect, it shows that the government acknowledges matewareware.nz's noble cause of mending the disparities in dementia care, actively working to close the gap in health outcomes through kotahitanga and fair resource distribution.

Article 3 - relation to the IT industry

Matewareware.nz is relevant to the IT industry, specifically ICT (Information Communication Technology), due to how it conveys information about dementia, by making a website dedicated to mate warware.

A website is very accessible since approximately 96% of New Zealand's population has access to the internet; therefore, information regarding mate wareware can be readily found online (Kemp, 2025). Due to the simplicity in access, it results in aiding Māori whānau, particularly for those who may face low health literacy, financial challenges, or limited access to services (remote living).

What is matewareware.nz?

Matewareware.nz is a web application developed with the collaborative effort of multiple licensed doctors, researchers, and organisations around New Zealand.

The objective of the application is to be informative and spread awareness of mate wareware. It does this by helping users navigate the sensitive subject of mate wareware (dementia) from both Māori and Pākehā perspectives, enriching the user’s over all worldview on the topic.

For instance, within the Māori context, mate wareware is perceived as a symptom of growing old; whereas, Pākehā deems mate wareware as a illness (Mate wareware, 2026).

In addition to helping users bridge the gap in understanding of mate wareware, the application provides proactive steps for your whānau to take in order to protect your kaumātua’s health, preventing the risk of getting mate wareware through simple lifestyle changes.

Visit matewareware.nz to learn more.

Kaumātua | Elders

Kaumātua can learn how to prevent or delay the progression of mate wareware by following the steps outlined on matewareware.nz. For example, kaumātua can make lifestyle changes such as reducing alcohol or tobacco intake and exercising at least 30 mins a day (Mate wareware, 2021).

Additionally, understanding the conditions can help strengthen their wairua, improving their quality of life.

Kaitiaki/Nēhi | Caregivers/Nurses

Kaitiaki shares matwareware.nz with their patient's whānau, enabling them to understand the symptoms of mate wareware and how to address it. Additionally, the application can be used to train junior kaitiaki to understand mate wareware in a Māori perspective.

Whānau | Family

Whānau, specifically mokopuna, can utilise matewareware.nz to understand their kaumātua change in behaviour due to mate wareware; therefore, enabling them to make informed decisions when caring for their kaumātua at home.

Te Tiriti o Waitangi | Partnership

Matewareware.nz was created in collaboration with doctors who specialises in dementia and Alzheimer's; university students, researchers, and organisations to compile their knowledge and spread awareness.

In relation to ITP's code of ethics (4 - Skills, 5 - Continuous Development, & 8 - Competence), the web developers and researchers are responsible to utilise their skill sets appropriately when developing the application. Furthermore, contributors to the website must continuously give their insight into mate wareware whenever new research on the condition is released.(IT Professionals NZ, 2015).

Te Tiriti o Waitangi | Participation

Historically, systems such as the Native Land Court stripped decision-making power from iwi and hapū. This app restores participation by returning health autonomy and tino rangatiratanga directly to the whānau.

Under ITP code of ethics (3 - Community Focus & 2 - Integrity), the developers of matewareware.nz must demonstrate their commitment of prioritising the community by preserving and integrating Māori world views when conveying information about mate wareware.

Furthermore, evidence of integrity is shown through the videos shown in their website, as it showcases tangible experiences from people who have either experienced or know someone with mate wareware (IT Professionals NZ, 2015).

Te Tiriti o Waitangi | Protection

Following the ITP code of ethics (6 - Informed Consent & 1 Good Faith), developers asked for consent when consulting kaumātuas about their experience and relationship with mate wareware. This can be seen through the interviews within the appliaction matwarewawre.nz (IT Professionals NZ, 2015).

Additionally, it is important that developers exhibit good faith and sincerity when building the app.
