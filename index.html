Protection

Tiaki

Protecting Māori Mātauranga

The Tohunga Suppression Act 1907 criminalized traditional healing; this app counters that legacy by reclaiming and centering Māori understandings of wellbeing and mate wareware.

Safeguards whānau wellbeing by providing health information that is culturally appropriate, ensuring that Māori are not forced to navigate systems that ignore their reo or tikanga.

Cultural & Health Protection

Digital & Health Equities

Historical policies created long-term disparities in health and resource access. providing culturally grounded digital tools, this app actively works to close those gaps and provide the protection of wellbeing guaranteed under Te Tiriti.

Articles

Article 2
Absolute Sovereignty
TINO RANGATIRATANGA

Māori having authority over their own knowledge and resources

Māori Perspective T he app is built in a Māori worldview, ensuring that mātauranga regarding mate wareware remains under the kaitiakitanga and authority of whānau.
Crown Perspective T he crown duty is to protect Māori interests and support self-determination. By resourcing a Māori-led digital platform, the Crown moves away from "one-size-fits-all" systems and fulfills its obligation to safeguard indigenous knowledge and autonomy in health.

IT Industry Relevancy

the application shows up in how the app is shaped from a Māori worldview rather than just adapting a mainstream tool. It reflects Māori ways of understanding health and ensures Māori perspectives are embedded in how the information is created and presented

Article 3
Equity
Ōritetanga

fairness in access and outcomes

Māori Perspective T he app breaks down barriers like low health literacy and service gaps. It ensures that dementia information is not just available, but is meaningful and easy to navigate, allowing whānau to care for their loved ones with dignity and support.
Crown Perspective T He crown guarantees equality and fairness By supporting a tool that targets specific disparities in dementia care. ensuring that health resources are accessible and effective for Māori, actively working to close the gap in health outcomes through kotahitanga and fair resource distribution.

IT Industry Relevancy

the app links to this by making dementia information more accessible and easier to engage with for Māori whānau (Family), particularly for those who may face barriers like health literacy challenges or limited access to services.

What is matewareware.nz

Matewareware.nz is an application developed with the collaborative effort of multiple licensed doctors, researchers, and organisations around New Zealand. The application is informative as it helps users navigate the sensitive subject of mate wareware (dementia) in the Māori and Pākehā (European) perspective, enriching the user’s over all world view on the topic. For instance, within the Māori context, mate wareware is perceived as a symptom of growing old; whereas, pākehā deems mate wareware as a illness. In addition to helping users bridge the gap in understanding of mate wareware, the application provides proactive steps for your whānau to take in order to protect your kaumātua’s health, preventing the risk of getting mate wareware through simple lifestyle changes. Visit matewareware.nz to learn more.

Kaumatua (Elders)

Kaumātua can learn how to prevent mate wareware or delay its progression further by following the steps cited in their website.
For example, Kaumātua can make lifestyle changes such as reducing alcohol or tobacco intake and exercising at least 30 mins a day.

Kaiwhakahaere/Nēhi (Caregivers/Nurses)

Kaiwhakahaere uses matewareware.nz by sharing the application with their patients who shows symptoms of mate wareware or their respective whānau to help them take care of their kaumātua.

Whānau (Family)

Whanau, specifically mokopuna (grandchild), can use matewareware.nz to understand their Kaumātua change in behaviour due to mate wareware and how to take care of them when encoutering difficult situations.

Te Tiriti o Waitangi | Partnership

matewareware.nz was created in collaboration of doctors specializing in dementia and Alzheimer; university student researchers, and organizations to compile their knowledge to spread awareness.

In relation to IITP's code of ethics (4, 5 & 8), the developers must be skilled and competent about mate wareware when developing the app. Members must continuously contribute their knowledge about mate wareware when there's new findings and

Te Tiriti o Waitangi | Participation

Restoring Participation: Historically, systems such as the Native Land Court stripped decision-making power from iwi and hapū. This app restores participation by returning health autonomy and tino rangatiratanga directly to the whānau.

Under ITP code of ethics (3 & 2), the developers of matewareware.nz demonstrates their commitment of prioritising the community by preserving and integrating Māori world views when conveying information about matewareware. Furthermore, integrity is shown through the videos shown in their website (IT Professionals NZ, 2015).

Te Tiriti o Waitangi | Protection

Following ITP code of ethics (6 & 1), developers made sure that when consulting kaumatuas, they ask them for their consent and be transparent on how they will store their data. more importantly that they show good faith and equality with no discriminatory and bias in the app.
